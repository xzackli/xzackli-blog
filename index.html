I'm Zack LiADS, a cosmologist. I think about the cosmic microwave background, the redshifted 21cm line, the large-scale structure of the Universe, and the nature of dark matter.

I am a postdoctoral fellow at UC Berkeley (BCCP) and a long-term visitor at CITA. A few things I'm thinking about:

  • Ultra-long-wavelength science from LuSEE-Night, a lunar farside radio telescope which will reach the moon in 2026
  • Design and calibration of interferometric radio cosmology experiments
  • New methods for extracting cosmological information from survey data

I spent two formative years as a postdoctoral fellow at the Canadian Institute for Theoretical Astrophysics (2021-2023, CITA). From 2016-2021, I obtained my PhD in astrophysics at Princeton, working with Jo Dunkley on data analysis for millimeter-wave surveys like ACT, SO, and CMB-S4. From 2012-2016, I studied math and physics at Swarthmore College.

I like new data, fast code, and crazy ideas.
